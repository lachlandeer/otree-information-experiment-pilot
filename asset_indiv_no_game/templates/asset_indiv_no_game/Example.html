Example Decision Problem

Below is an example of the decision screen. In each decision problem, you will see three signals and the mean of all potential asset values (Mean Value).

Signal 1 Signal 2 Signal 3 Mean Value
Information 98 103 99 100
Allocation of Tokens

In this example, the three signals show 98, 103 and 99. You will be given 100 tokens to allocate towards each of the four pieces of information. The more tokens you assign to a certain piece of information, the more important you consider it is when estimating the target value:

Target Value = the Value of the Asset (V)

To form an estimate of the target value, you need to input your allocated tokens into their respective boxes.

Signal 1 Signal 2 Signal 3 Mean Value
Information 98 103 99 100
Allocation of Tokens 35 10 35 20

Estimated Target Value

Your estimate of the target value is 99.25

In this example, you have 35 tokens allocated to 98, 10 tokens to 103, 35 tokens to 99, and 20 tokens to the mean value. Your guess of the target value is calculated by adding up the weighted values of each allocation. The formula used is: (35% × 98) + (10% × 103) + (35% × 99) + (20% × 100) = 99.25.

You can adjust your allocation of the tokens until you have optimized your allocation and are satisfied with your estimate.

Your earnings

The closer your estimate is to the Target Value, the higher your payoff from this decision problem will be. The payoff will be calculated according to the formula below:

500 – (Your Estimate – Target Value)2

You can expect to earn more by forming an estimate that is closer to the Target Value because the deduction is smaller the closer your estimate is to the Target Value (V).

The feedback screen will show (your allocation of tokens,) your estimate of the Target Value, the target value and your earnings in that decision problem.

In this example, suppose V is 105. Then the feedback screen will show the following information:

Your Choices

Note that your earnings are calculated as follows: 500 – (99.25– 105)2 = 466.93 points
